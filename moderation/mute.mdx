## Overview

The `mute` command silences a member across all channels in your server. Muted members cannot send messages, react, or speak in voice channels.

## Usage
$mute @user [duration] [reason]

Or with a slash command:

/mute user: @user duration: [duration] reason: [reason]

## Options

| Option | Required | Description |
|---|---|---|
| `user` | ✅ | The member to mute |
| `duration` | ❌ | How long to mute (e.g. `1h`, `30m`, `1d`) |
| `reason` | ❌ | The reason for the mute |

## Duration Format

| Format | Example | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Minutes | `30m` | 30 minutes |
| Hours | `1h` | 1 hour |
| Days | `1d` | 1 day |
| Permanent | *(leave blank)* | Until manually unmuted |

## Examples
$mute @alexxxzdevelops 1h Spamming

$mute @alexxxzdevelops 30m

$mute @alexxxzdevelops

/mute user:@alexxxzdevelops duration:1h reason:Spamming

## Behavior

- The muted member will receive a DM notification with the duration and reason
- The action will be logged in your mod log channel if configured
- The mute is recorded in Aegis's database and visible in the dashboard
- If a duration is set, Aegis will automatically unmute the member when it expires

## Unmuting a Member

To manually unmute a member before the duration expires:
$unmute @user

<Warning>
  You cannot mute members with a higher or equal role than yours.
</Warning>

<Info>
  For Discord's native timeout feature, see the [Timeout](/moderation/timeout) command instead.
</Info>
